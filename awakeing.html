Overview

The Second Great Awakening was a religious revival movement in the United States during the late 1700s and early 1800s. It spread quickly across frontier regions where churches were limited.

Key Features

  • Large outdoor camp meetings
  • Emotional preaching styles
  • Preachers traveling from town to town
  • Focus on personal salvation

Impact

The movement encouraged people to believe they could improve themselves and society, which led to major reform movements.
