Overview

The Second Great Awakening was a religious movement in the United States during the late 1700s and early 1800s. It spread quickly across frontier areas where churches were limited, and traveling preachers held large outdoor meetings known as camp meetings.

During the same period, Unitarianism developed mainly in New England. It offered a different view of religion, focusing more on reason, education, and moral behavior instead of emotional revival experiences.

This website explains both movements and shows how they influenced each other, as well as how they led to major reform movements in American society.

What This Site Covers

  • What the Second Great Awakening was and how it spread
  • What Unitarianism believed and how it differed
  • How the two movements disagreed about religion
  • How both influenced reform movements like abolition and education

Big Idea

One major difference between these movements was style. The Second Great Awakening focused on emotional preaching and conversion experiences, while Unitarianism focused on calm thinking and personal improvement over time. Together, they shaped how Americans viewed religion and reform.
