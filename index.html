Welcome

In the early 1800s, America experienced major religious change. Two important movements — the Second Great Awakening and Unitarianism — shaped how people thought about faith, morality, and society.

The Second Great Awakening

A powerful religious revival movement that spread across the United States, especially on the frontier. It emphasized emotional preaching, personal salvation, and camp meetings.

Unitarianism

A religious movement centered in New England that focused on reason, education, and moral improvement rather than emotional conversion experiences.

Why it matters

These movements didn’t just change religion — they helped inspire major reforms like abolition, women’s rights, and education reform.
