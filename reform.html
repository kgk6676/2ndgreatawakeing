Overview

The Second Great Awakening led to many reform movements because it encouraged people to improve themselves and society.

Main Reform Movements

  • Abolition Movement – ending slavery
  • Women’s Rights Movement – expanding rights and roles for women
  • Temperance Movement – reducing alcohol use
  • Education Reform – improving public schooling

Why It Happened

People believed that personal faith should lead to positive action in society. This idea helped motivate reform efforts across the United States.
